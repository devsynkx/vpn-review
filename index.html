Why people use VPNs

Speed & stability

A good VPN keeps your connection stable and fast enough for browsing, streaming and video calls. Actual speeds will always depend on your ISP, distance to the server and network load.

Security & privacy

VPNs encrypt your internet traffic, which helps protect your data on public Wi-Fi and adds an extra layer of privacy against tracking and basic surveillance.

Access & content

Many people use VPNs to access region-locked content, websites or services. Keep in mind that availability can change, and you should always follow the terms of each platform.

FAQ — common VPN questions

Do I really need a VPN?
It depends on how you use the internet. Many people get a VPN to add an extra layer of privacy, secure public Wi-Fi, or access streaming libraries and websites that are not available in their region.

Is using a VPN legal in the United States?
In most countries, including the US, using a VPN itself is legal. However, illegal activities remain illegal whether you use a VPN or not. Always follow the laws of your country and the terms of each service you use.

Does a VPN always work with Netflix or other streaming platforms?
No VPN can guarantee permanent access to any specific streaming service. Platforms constantly update their systems, and availability can change. Check each provider’s website or recent user reviews for the latest streaming compatibility information.

Will a VPN slow down my internet?
Some speed loss is normal because your traffic is being encrypted and routed through an extra server. Modern protocols like WireGuard or NordLynx usually keep the impact small, especially on good connections.

Can my ISP still see what I’m doing online?
With a VPN enabled, your ISP typically sees that you’re connected to a VPN server, but not which specific websites or services you access. However, you should still choose a trustworthy VPN provider with a clear privacy policy.

Do VPNs keep logs of my activity?
Many premium VPNs advertise "no-logs" policies, but the details differ. It’s important to read each provider’s privacy policy and look for independent audits that verify their claims.

Can I use one VPN subscription on multiple devices?
Yes. Most VPNs allow several simultaneous connections. Surfshark, for example, offers unlimited devices per subscription at the time of writing.

Is a VPN enough to make me completely anonymous?
No. A VPN is just one privacy tool. It does not protect you from all types of tracking, malware, account leaks or unsafe browsing behavior. You still need strong passwords, updates and common-sense online habits.

Can I use a VPN for torrenting?
Some VPNs support P2P traffic on specific servers. If you plan to torrent, check whether the provider allows it and what restrictions apply in your country. Always respect copyright laws.
